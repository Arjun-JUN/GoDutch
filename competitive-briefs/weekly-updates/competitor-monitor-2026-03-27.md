### Regulatory & Industry Trends

- **EU PSD3 / PSR**: Provisional political agreement reached Nov 2025. Formal adoption pending. Will enter into force with a 21-month transition — impacts all European P2P apps, including Tricount (which is EU-based via bunq).
- **EU Instant Payments Regulation (IPR)**: First mandatory PSP compliance reporting due April 2026 — real-time euro payments becoming standard.
- **US Genius Act stablecoin rules**: Multiple agency rule-making deadlines set for July 18, 2026. P2P apps using stablecoins will face new compliance requirements.
- **IRS 1099-K P2P reporting**: New requirements for P2P networks (Venmo, PayPal, Cash App) began in 2025 — platforms now required to report transactions above $600 to the IRS. Creates friction for casual users that could push some toward simpler split-and-settle apps.
- **ISO 20022**: Deadline to remove unstructured addresses from cross-border payments messages coming in November 2026 — operational compliance requirement for any app touching international transfers.
- **General regulatory shift in 2026**: Focus moving from rule-making to enforcement, operational resilience, and data quality scrutiny.

### AI / OCR Innovation Watch

- OCR accuracy for receipt scanning has reached **95%+ field-level accuracy** in 2026 (vendor names, dates, amounts, tax lines) — this is the baseline user expectation GoDutch must meet.
- **Veryfi**: AI-powered OCR API extracting structured data from receipts in real time — popular for custom enterprise workflows. Could be a GoDutch integration candidate.
- **Yomio**: Consumer AI receipt scanner that auto-logs every item, price, and category — no manual entry required. A consumer-grade benchmark.
- **SparkReceipt**: Processes documents in 2–3 seconds, extracts merchant names, dates, totals, taxes, currencies. Up to 95% accuracy.
- **Expensify**: TrustRadius Buyer's Choice 2026 winner; strong on AI-powered receipt OCR + corporate card + reimbursement workflows. Setting enterprise expectations.
- **Trend**: Leading platforms (Klippa SpendControl, Zoho Expense) now use AI not just to scan but to *predict and suggest* expense entries based on user behavior patterns.
- **Fraud detection** via ML is becoming standard — apps flag spending outliers automatically.

## Strategic Implications for GoDutch

1. **The "free tier" gap is GoDutch's most immediate go-to-market lever.** Splitwise's aggressively paywalled free tier is the #1 complaint driving user churn. GoDutch can capture this audience by offering genuinely unlimited free expense logging *with* OCR scanning — the feature Splitwise charges for. This is a powerful acquisition narrative: "Everything Splitwise Pro gives you, free."

2. **Item-level receipt splitting is unowned whitespace.** Every competitor either lacks receipt OCR or fails to do item-level assignment (who gets the steak vs. the salad). This specific feature combination — scan a receipt, tap to assign line items to individuals — would be a differentiated, viral demo moment. Prioritize this in the OCR spec.

3. **SplitterUp is a direct 2025-launched competitor with similar positioning — monitor weekly.** They're leading with AI scanning + free-forever pricing. GoDutch needs to know their user traction and any feature releases before launch to ensure differentiation holds.

4. **Voice input as a differentiator is not yet commoditized.** No major competitor offers natural-language voice logging of expenses. GoDutch's voice input capability is genuinely novel in this space — lean into it in marketing and UX.

5. **Tricount's post-acquisition instability is an opening.** Since bunq acquired Tricount, sync issues and removed premium features have eroded trust. Users who loved Tricount's simplicity are actively looking for alternatives — GoDutch should consider positioning as the "reliable, modern Tricount" for European users as well.

6. **Regulatory compliance should be baked in early.** The IRS 1099-K reporting rules, EU PSD3, and US stablecoin regulations are converging. If GoDutch plans any payment settlement features (not just tracking), legal and compliance review should begin now, especially for any EU market ambitions.
